**Overview**
Convolutional neural networks (CNNs) are a type of artificial neural network that have become increasingly popular in image recognition tasks due to their ability to extract features from images.

**Key Findings**
Research has shown that CNNs use convolutional layers to extract features from images, which are then combined using pooling layers to reduce spatial dimensions [S6]. The depth of the convolutional network also plays a crucial role in its accuracy, with deeper networks often achieving better performance on large-scale image recognition tasks [S6].

**Cross-Modal Analysis**
The key to CNNs' success lies in their ability to respect the spatial structure of input data. By defining new operators that can operate on images or spatially structured data, neural networks can effectively extract features from images [S7]. This is particularly important for image recognition tasks, where the spatial relationships between pixels are crucial.

**Conclusion**
In conclusion, CNNs use convolutional layers to extract features from images, which are then combined using pooling layers. The depth of the network also plays a critical role in its accuracy. By respecting the spatial structure of input data, neural networks can effectively extract features from images, making them a powerful tool for image recognition tasks.
